# Rachel Moore

Founder of Sagebeam. 10-year product management background. AI-native builder — not a developer; builds with AI tools, strong product instincts. Based in San Francisco; has lived in California for 16 years. Grew up in South Carolina. [Source: User, brand foundation session, 2026-06-22]

## Personal Context

Long-distance family caregiver. Parents live in South Carolina; Rachel lives 2,500 miles away in San Francisco. Mom has had cancer multiple times, a TIA, and a heart condition. Dad had emergency triple bypass surgery. Both still independent, mobile, and cognitively intact, but hospitalized approximately once per year. [Source: User, 2026-06-22]

Key moments:
- Rachel was alone with her mom during a cardiac episode (dad on a business trip) and had to call 911 — the founding image of the problem she's solving. [Source: User, verbatim context, 2026-06-22]
- Dad had two separate ER visits where doctors suspected sepsis — Rachel found out years later, just last week, not because anyone called her, but because she's building a company and it came up in conversation. [Source: User, 2026-06-22]
- Dad refused rehab and home nursing post-bypass due to cost uncertainty. Rachel didn't know enough at the time to push back. She knows now what that decision probably cost him in recovery. [Source: User, 2026-06-22]

Also navigates the system as a patient herself: one kidney removed (non-functional), remaining kidney slowly degrading, other chronic and still-unexplained health issues including chronically low platelet count. [Source: User, 2026-06-22]

Rachel and her parents are comfortable being publicly referenced in Sagebeam's story. Rachel's framing: "this is a business that could make a lot of money and that is the price of success." [Source: User, verbatim, 2026-06-22]

## Professional Background

- 10 years in product management prior to founding Sagebeam
- AI-native builder; uses LLMs as core development tools
- Solo founder

## Brand Goals

Primary: inbound relationships and visibility with healthcare decision-makers — hospital, MA plan, specialty group, and outpatient leadership.
Secondary: relevant non-profits and thought leaders.
Third: investors and founders.

LinkedIn is the relationship-building surface for operators and buyers. X is the signal surface for capital and peer networks. [Source: User, brand foundation session, 2026-06-22]
