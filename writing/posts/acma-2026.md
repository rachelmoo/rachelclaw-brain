## Post 1 — Pre-conference (FINAL, approved 2026-06-23)

> I've been to a lot of conferences lately. The one I'm most looking forward to is two days with hospital case managers, social workers, and care transition teams at the American Case Management Association (ACMA) - South Carolina Chapter conference this weekend.
>
> Most of the rooms I sit in are full of founders and investors talking about healthcare. ACMA is full of the people who carry it - the ones doing the discharge planning, working the handoff, sitting across from a family that isn't ready to take someone home. That's where the real understanding of the transition out of the hospital lives.
>
> I've lived the family side of this for years: my parents have had health issues for the last decade-plus, with some kind of ER visit at least once a year. But the professional craft - what social workers and case managers actually do every day - I haven't done. These two days, I want to listen to the people who have.
>
> If you'll be there, I'd like to hear what you're up against right now: the discharge prep that falls apart, the handoffs that don't hold, the part of the job that's quietly gotten harder. Grab me for a coffee.
