# Engagement & Distribution Playbook — Rachel Moore / Sagebeam

How Rachel builds relationships and visibility, distinct from WHAT she posts (see [concepts/brand-pillars.md](../concepts/brand-pillars.md)) and her POSITIONING (see [concepts/brand-positioning.md](../concepts/brand-positioning.md)). [Source: brand foundation session, 2026-06-22]

## Core principle

**For the primary goal, engagement is the job. Content is just the credibility layer that makes the engagement land.** Most founders invert this — post constantly, engage minimally. For hospital and MA-plan leadership (relationship buyers), that fails: they need to recognize Rachel's name before they'll take a meeting. [Source: brand foundation session, verbatim principle]

## LinkedIn engagement stack

1. **Who to target** — people who are *active* on LinkedIn, not just senior. An active CMO with 2k followers who posts and replies beats a silent one with 20k. Roles: VPs of Care Management / Care Transitions / Value-Based Care; CMOs, CNOs, Medical Directors; MA-plan Directors of Clinical Operations; post-acute & ambulatory leadership; large specialty-group practice administrators.
2. **How to find them** — LinkedIn search by title + org type, filtered for *activity*; follow target hashtags (#TEAMmodel, #bundledpayments, #caretransitions, #carecoordination, #populationhealth, #valuebasedcare, #heartfailure, #alzheimers); find the active voices in the comment sections of NEJM Catalyst, AHIP, ACMA, AHA; CMS publishes the TEAM-participating hospital list = the Tier-1 outbound target list.
3. **The comment is the highest-leverage tool.** A good comment adds a specific data point, shares a real observation ("in talking to care managers, I keep hearing…"), asks a genuine advancing question, or respectfully adds a nuance. Rule: *if the comment could be copy-pasted onto any post about any topic, delete it.* Goal = be the most interesting reply on the post; do that consistently on someone's content and they notice.
4. **Connection sequencing** — never cold-connect. Engage 2-3× with substantive comments first, *then* connect with a short personalized note referencing something specific. By then it's not cold.
5. **DM sequencing** — only after comment presence is established; lead with genuine curiosity, not a pitch ("genuinely curious how your team is thinking about [Y]" — not "I'd love to show you what we're building"). The ask comes later.
6. **Own-post engagement** — respond to every comment on your posts, especially early. Lurkers watch this; it signals a real person, not a broadcast account.
7. **The ratio** — ~60-70% of LinkedIn time on others' content, 30-40% on your own. Building relationships, not an audience.

## Time budget — 10-15 hrs/week

Rachel's cap: "as much as it takes before diminishing returns; realistically not more than 10-15 hrs." [Source: User, verbatim]

| Activity | Time/week |
|---|---|
| Monitoring targets + finding new ones | 1-2 hrs |
| Engaging on others' content (comments) | 4-5 hrs ← protect this if anything gives |
| Own content creation + scheduling | 2-3 hrs |
| Connection requests + DMs | 1 hr |
| X (content + engagement) | 2 hrs |

## Tracking (keep simple — a sheet, not a CRM yet)

- **Warm targets:** name, org, role, last touchpoint, status (following → commented → connected → DM'd → meeting)
- **Content calendar:** post type, topic, platform, date
- **Watchlist:** hashtags/accounts to check weekly

## X — three modes (not one)

Rachel flagged X was underweighted and named the modes herself. [Source: User, 2026-06-22]

1. **Healthcare thesis** — sharp, citable takes on the system, the CMS squeeze, caregiver burden. Same intellectual content as LinkedIn, punchier. Builds credibility with healthcare people, shows investors she knows the domain cold.
2. **Founder in real-time** — what it's actually like to build in 2026; AI-native product development, what's working/broke/surprised. The content that makes investors want to meet her before she cold-emails. Honest specific dispatches, not "lessons learned" listicles.
3. **SF embedded** — events, observations, running into people (HLTH, JPM, startup events). Ambient social proof she's in the ecosystem and moving.

**Solo founding stance:** don't lead with it as an identity, don't dodge it. Let it come up naturally ("as someone building this without a technical co-founder, here's what I've learned about…"). Don't make "solo founder grind" the personality — it undercuts the strategic/domain-credibility angle. [Source: session take, 2026-06-22]

**X time (2-3 hrs/week):** Mode 1 ~1 post every few days; Mode 2 when something's worth sharing; Mode 3 as it occurs; 20-30 min/day scanning + 1-2 good comments to active health-tech investors/founders. On X, own content matters more than engagement volume (flips from LinkedIn). Don't cross-post from LinkedIn — different voice/format.
