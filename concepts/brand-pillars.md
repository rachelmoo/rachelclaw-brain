# Brand Pillars — Rachel Moore / Sagebeam

Five durable content pillars. Each survives "50 posts without faking it." [Source: brand foundation session, 2026-06-22]

---

## Pillar 1: The Caregiver as Clinical Asset

**POV:** The family caregiver is healthcare's most underused lever. Every piece of rigorous transitional care research points to this. The system has known it for decades and done almost nothing about it — not because the evidence was absent, but because the delivery mechanism didn't exist.

**Why spiky:** Challenges the default assumption that caregivers are peripheral to clinical outcomes. Reframes them as a core variable in readmissions, medication adherence, and recovery.

**Format inventory:**
- Research synthesis (TCM, PACE, PWD caregiver studies)
- Reframe posts ("The care team doesn't end at discharge")
- Data-backed arguments connecting caregiver engagement to clinical metrics
- Responses to the "caregivers aren't reliable" objection

**Primary audience:** LinkedIn skeptics and believers-without-solutions; hospital/MA plan operators

---

## Pillar 2: The ROI Is Real (But Hidden)

**POV:** Healthcare operators assume care coordination doesn't have ROI. They're wrong — but the failure wasn't the concept, it was the delivery model. Manual coordination couldn't scale, couldn't engage caregivers asynchronously, and couldn't capture data. The ROI case was always there; the mechanism to realize it wasn't.

**Why spiky:** Directly challenges the dominant belief of the buyer audience. Doesn't concede the premise; reframes it.

**Format inventory:**
- CMS/bundled payment pressure (TEAM model, CAHPS, Stars)
- Readmission cost math
- "Why the ROI case was invisible" explanatory posts
- Proof point posts (50% discharge instruction recall stat, 20% med rec readmissions)

**Primary audience:** LinkedIn skeptics; hospital CFOs, quality directors, VBP leads

---

## Pillar 3: The War on Caregivers

**POV:** The system is structurally offloading more onto families — hospital at home, shorter lengths of stay, reduced follow-up — with zero support. This isn't accidental neglect; it's the predictable output of cost pressure and misaligned incentives. Most caregivers are women. Many quit their jobs, surrender their economic independence, and spend their last healthy years caring for someone else before they get sick themselves.

**Why spiky:** Names the structural problem as a "war" — not a gap or an oversight. Connects healthcare policy to gender equity and economic participation.

**Rachel's exact phrasing:** "it's like an epidemic/war on caregivers that no one is talking about" [Source: User, verbatim, 2026-06-22]

**Format inventory:**
- Systemic observation posts
- Workforce and economic angle (women, job loss, economic freedom)
- Personal narrative (used sparingly, with purpose)
- "Nobody is talking about this" framing

**Primary audience:** LinkedIn all segments and X; non-profits; policymakers; potential viral reach

---

## Pillar 4: What Good Actually Looks Like

**POV:** Most "caregiver support" tools fail because they imagine caregivers who have time, a smartphone, and a login. The alternative isn't a portal nobody uses — it's something that meets caregivers where they already are, handles real complexity, and doesn't require a care manager to manage it.

**Why spiky:** Shifts from "we need to help caregivers" (broadly agreed, broadly ignored) to "here's what help actually looks like operationally."

**Format inventory:**
- SMS-first rationale
- Operational specificity ("what breaks at 11pm post-discharge")
- "What I've learned from talking to caregivers" posts
- Contrast posts (what doesn't work and why)

**Primary audience:** LinkedIn believers-without-solutions; post-acute and ambulatory leadership; case managers

---

## Pillar 5: Building in 2026 (X-primary)

**POV:** LLMs make a previously unbuildable class of product now buildable. AI-native founding in 2026 is a genuinely different experience — different tools, different product loops, different leverage. Building this without a technical co-founder is both harder and more possible than it would have been three years ago.

**Why spiky:** Specific to this moment. Not "AI is transforming healthcare" (generic) but what it actually looks like to build this product right now.

**Format inventory:**
- Founder dispatch posts (what I tried, what broke, what worked)
- "Why LLMs unlock this specifically" (investor audience)
- Solo-founding-in-2026 moments (mentioned naturally, not as identity)
- SF embedded: events, conversations, observations

**Primary audience:** X (investors, founders); secondary LinkedIn
