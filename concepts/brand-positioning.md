# Brand Positioning — Rachel Moore / Sagebeam

## The Bet

"The only founder building intelligent, SMS-first caregiver support for the 59M Americans caregiving blind — who is also a long-distance caregiver and chronic illness patient herself."

The lane: credible operator-turned-founder who lives the problem from both sides (caregiver and patient), with the product insight that LLMs finally unlock what manual coordination never could. [Source: User + compiled, brand foundation session, 2026-06-22]

## Content Thesis

> "The family caregiver is the most underused lever in care delivery — and the reason nobody could prove the ROI before is that the old manual model was structurally incapable of engaging them at scale."

[Source: compiled from User, 2026-06-22]

## Origin Arc

Personal and structural. Rachel has been a long-distance caregiver for parents with serious, recurring health issues for 10+ years. She called 911 alone for her mom's cardiac episode while her dad was traveling. She found out years later — last week — that her dad had two ER visits where doctors suspected sepsis; no one called her. Her dad refused rehab post-bypass because he didn't trust cost estimates; she didn't know enough to push back. She also navigates the system as a chronic illness patient herself.

When she looked for tools that existed for caregivers in her situation, she found almost nothing — despite 59M Americans being in the same position. LLMs changed what was buildable. She built Sagebeam. [Source: User, 2026-06-22]

## Differentiation

- **Lived experience:** both caregiver (long-distance, recurring parental crises) and patient (chronic illness, system navigation)
- **Operator knowledge:** 10 years PM; understands healthcare buyer workflows and incentives
- **Technical insight:** AI-native builder; first to credibly apply LLMs to this specific delivery problem
- **Mission authenticity:** "There's a war on caregivers in this country that nobody is talking about" — visceral, specific, grounded in her own story [Source: User, verbatim, 2026-06-22]

## What Doesn't Work

Avoid founder-pitching-operators register. Hospital and MA plan leadership are skeptical of vendor founders. Content must feel like it comes from someone who lives in their world, not someone who wants something from them. [Source: brand foundation session, 2026-06-22]

## Platform-Specific Positioning

**LinkedIn:** Thought leader for healthcare decision-makers. Positioned as the person who synthesizes and contextualizes existing evidence on caregiver leverage. Domain credibility first; product second. LLM/AI framing deprioritized for this audience — lead with outcomes.

**X:** Signal to capital and peer networks. Founder conviction + domain expertise + SF-embedded. "War on caregivers" framing; LLM unlock angle; founder-in-real-time dispatches. AI framing welcome here.
