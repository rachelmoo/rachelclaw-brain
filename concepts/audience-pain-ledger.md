# Audience Pain Ledger — Rachel Moore / Sagebeam

[Source: brand foundation session, 2026-06-22]

---

## Primary Target Audience

Healthcare decision-makers with post-discharge accountability and financial skin in the game:

- **Tier 1:** TEAM-participating hospitals (VP Care Management, VP Value-Based Care, Quality Directors, CMOs); cardiology practices and cardiac surgery groups; ASC leadership doing cardiac procedures
- **Tier 2:** MA plan clinical operations and care management leadership; neurology practices; geriatricians
- **Tier 3 (visibility/credibility):** Non-profits (AARP, Alzheimer's Association, AHA, National Alliance for Caregiving, ACMA); thought leaders in transitional care, aging policy, care management

## Anti-Audience

- Consumer-facing caregiving apps (different buyer, different model)
- Staffing/home care agencies (different solution category)
- General public
- Anyone who believes care coordination ROI is irrelevant to their business

---

## Three Buyer Segments

### Segment 1: Skeptics
**Belief:** "The caregiver is not a reliable clinical or financial lever."
**Pain:** Readmissions penalties, TEAM bundled payment pressure, Stars/HEDIS gaps — attributed to clinical factors, not caregiver engagement gaps.
**What moves them:** ROI data first. Hard numbers. CMS pressure framing. Don't lead with the solution; lead with evidence that the problem is financially material.
**Language:** "We've tried care coordination before." "Show me the ROI." "Our care managers are already overwhelmed."

### Segment 2: Believers-Without-Solutions
**Belief:** "We know we need to do something for caregivers, but we don't know what would actually work."
**Pain:** Manual coordination fails at scale. Portals no one uses. Open to something new but skeptical it'll be different.
**What moves them:** Operational specificity. Show what "help" actually looks like. SMS-first directly addresses the "portal nobody uses" failure.
**Language:** "We tried a patient portal but adoption was terrible." "Our social workers are burned out." "We just don't have capacity."

### Segment 3: Waiting-for-Someone
**Belief:** "The caregiver is a lever and someone needs to build the right tool."
**Pain:** They've been waiting. May have tried to build internally or fund something that didn't work.
**What moves them:** Visibility and credibility. They need to know Rachel and Sagebeam exist.
**Language:** "We've been thinking about this for years." "Nothing we've seen has been quite right."

## Universal Pains

- Readmissions penalties under value-based payment contracts
- TEAM model (Jan 2026): mandatory bundled payments for CABG and other episodes; 90-day post-discharge financial accountability
- Post-discharge "black hole" — hospital does everything right, patient goes home, then silence
- Care manager and social worker shortage/burnout; manual coordination doesn't scale
- Stars ratings and HEDIS affected by post-discharge medication adherence and follow-up gaps
- Hospital at home and reduced LOS pushing more onto families with less preparation

## Universal Desires

- Scalable solution that doesn't add burden to care management staff
- Something patients/caregivers will actually use
- Measurable outcomes for finance/C-suite
- Low-friction integration with existing workflows
- Vendor who understands the clinical and operational context

## False Beliefs to Challenge

- "Care coordination doesn't have ROI" → ROI exists; the delivery model didn't
- "Family caregivers aren't reliable intervention points" → most proximate person to patient post-discharge
- "Technology won't work for this population" → SMS-first removes the friction that killed every portal
- "This is a nice-to-have" → under TEAM, it's a balance sheet item

## Language Map

| Their term | What it signals |
|---|---|
| Readmissions, 30-day readmission rate | Core financial and quality metric |
| Transitions of care, care transitions | The handoff problem Sagebeam solves |
| Post-discharge | The specific window Sagebeam operates in |
| SDOH | Broadens to non-clinical caregiver needs |
| Stars ratings, HEDIS | MA plan quality measures affected by caregiver gaps |
| Value-based care, risk-based contracts | Financial model creating the incentive |
| Bundled payments, TEAM model | CMS pressure creating urgency now |
| Care coordination, case management | The manual incumbent model |
| Patient activation, patient engagement | Related KPIs; caregiver engagement is a proxy |
| LOS, throughput | Upstream pressure that creates the discharge problem |
