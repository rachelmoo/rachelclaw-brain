# Sagebeam

SMS-first caregiving support platform. Helps family caregivers navigate the post-discharge and ongoing care journey — medically, legally, financially, emotionally, logistically. [Source: User, brand foundation session, 2026-06-22]

## Founder

Rachel Moore. See [personal/rachel.md](../personal/rachel.md).

## Problem

59 million Americans are caring for a loved one with two or more chronic conditions. 10,000 people turn 65 every day in the US. The system is offloading more and more onto family caregivers — hospital at home, shorter lengths of stay, reduced follow-up — with no tools, no knowledge, and no support. Most caregivers are women; many quit their jobs, sacrifice their economic freedom, and their own health. [Source: User, 2026-06-22]

Existing tools are severely lacking to the point of being nonexistent. [Source: User, verbatim, 2026-06-22]

## Solution

SMS-first. Meets caregivers where they are. Intelligent enough to handle real complexity. Does not require a care manager to manage it. LLMs unlock this class of product — not previously buildable with manual or rule-based approaches. [Source: User, 2026-06-22]

## Why SMS-First

Post-discharge manual care coordination structurally cannot engage family caregivers at scale: not present at 2am, can't handle the breadth of non-clinical questions, can't capture data. SMS reaches caregivers in their existing behavior without requiring app downloads, portals, or new workflows. [Source: User, brand foundation session, 2026-06-22]

## ICP

- **Tier 1:** TEAM-participating hospitals (CABG and other cardiac episodes under mandatory bundled payment pressure, launched Jan 2026); cardiology practices and cardiac surgery groups; ASC leadership doing cardiac procedures
- **Tier 2:** MA plan care management leadership; neurology practices (stroke, Parkinson's — high caregiver burden); geriatricians
- **Tier 3 (visibility):** Non-profits (AARP, Alzheimer's Association, AHA, National Alliance for Caregiving, ACMA); thought leaders in transitional care and aging policy

Disease focus: cardiac diseases (CHF, CAD, CABG recovery, AFIB) and Alzheimer's/related dementia. [Source: User, 2026-06-22]

## Why Now

1. CMS TEAM model — mandatory bundled payments for selected hospitals including CABG; hospitals actively looking for post-discharge solutions right now.
2. LLMs make the product previously unbuildable now buildable.
3. Exponentially growing TAM (10k turning 65/day, accelerating caregiver burden).
[Source: User, 2026-06-22]
